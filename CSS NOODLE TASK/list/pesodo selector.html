CHENNAI

Known as the gateway to South India, Chennai is the capital of Tamil Nadu. Chennai is a sprawling, busy and conservative city with deep traditions that are yet to give space to the rising western influence. Chennai doesn’t have well-renowned monuments or tourist attractions like some other cities in India yet, if you are willing to explore Chennai, it will show you why it is so special among travelers.

MADURAI

Home to the most important and impressive temple in South India, Meenakshi Temple, Madurai is an ancient city in Tamil Nadu. If you have to choose just one south Indian temple to visit, then Meenakshi Temple is the right choice for you. Madurai city dates back to almost 4,000 years and was a major center of Tamil learnings and culture. Many magnificent temples and religious buildings with intricate architecture were built during the rule of the Nayak dynasty. In April, Madurai hosts a 12-day Chithirai Festival, featuring a re-enacted celestial wedding of idols. .

COVAI

Located around two hours away from Madurai, Tamil Nadu’s Chettinad region is popular for its fiery meat curries and old mansions. A day trip to Chettinad or a heritage stay at a mansion are the perfect ways to explore this region. The food here is the major highlight of Chettinad; a meal including seven courses is served on a banana leaf. This place offers cooking classes and special packages for the exploration of local cuisine.
